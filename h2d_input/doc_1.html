Training and support

Oncourse CL, IU's online collaboration and learning environment, powered by the Sakai community, supports teaching and learning, committees, projects, research, and portfolios for Indiana University's community of students, faculty, and staff.

You can get help using Oncourse CL in many ways. In addition to the many resources you see listed below, after clicking the Help tool in Oncourse CL, you'll see two frames on the left of the window:

  • Help search tool: In the upper left frame, type Oncourse CL search terms (e.g., reordering site tabs), and then click Search.
  • Expandable index: In the lower left frame, click the plus sign (+) to see topics. Then click to choose a topic or tool.

You can also get help with your current tool by clicking the question mark (?) in the upper right corner of the tool window.

  • Getting started:
    • Logging into Oncourse CL: If you are not already logged in when you are finished with the Help tool, close its window and, in the upper right corner of the Oncourse CL portal, click Login. When prompted, type your IU username and passphrase. Once you're logged in, you'll see your courses in tabs across the top, and a list of tools on the left. (If you do not see your course, you may need to click more on the far right of your tabs.)
    • Oncourse CL changes and enhancements for 2007-2008 - A summary of development throughout the 2007-2008 academic year
    • Oncourse Transition Guide - See which tools in Oncourse CL best match the way you used original Oncourse.
    • Oncourse CL Quick Start Guide: For Faculty - This guide (in PDF format) is designed to help faculty and other instructors prepare to use Oncourse CL to support their courses.
    • Oncourse CL Quick Start Guide: For Students - This guide (in PDF format) is designed to help students learn how to use Oncourse CL for their course work.

  • Tips to make Oncourse CL work for you - Tips for teaching, learning, and collaborating with Oncourse CL
  • Self study materials:
    • Feature demonstrations - 2-4 minute video tutorials of Oncourse CL features
    • The least you need to know (for instructors) - Simple, basic step-by-step instructions
    • In-depth self-study materials for instructors - Focusing on common teaching tasks and tools, there are eight of these printable 40- to 80-page materials that provide step-by-step instructions.
  • Workshops - Most workshops are 60-90 minutes long, are led by an Oncourse CL expert, and provide hands-on experience on pedagogical tools. They are free to all IU faculty, students, and staff.
  • Campus teaching and learning centers - The campus centers are ready to support instructors as they integrate Oncourse CL into their teaching.
  • Request a site - IU instructors and staff may request practice course sites or project sites.
  • Get application help 24/7 by email, phone, or walk-in from your campus support center.
